File Format

I used a Canon camera to shoot my short film. This camera encodes recordings into a MOV file. MOV decoders on some browsers are imperfect, as they cannot play every video. To this end, I reformatted the file to MPEG-4 with 720p resolution because this file format has full support across all browsers, guaranteeing a consistent experience.

In addition, another motivation to reformat was conserving disk space. The original MOV file consumes 343.9 MegaBytes (MB), whereas its converted counterpart consumes only 32.5 MB. This considerable reduction in storage requirements renders it a more viable option for use on the web.

Film Editing

The list of steps below describe the procedure to reproduce my film.

  1. Import the necessary video assets into a new project:
    • Raw footage;
    • Monkeys spinning monkeys background music;
    • Captions
  2. Play only the first minute of the audio track while the film is playing. Strip the rest of the audio sequence and remove it from the project.
  3. Add a fade-in effect at the beginning of audio track lasting five (5) seconds.
  4. Fade out the audio at the end for five (5) seconds.
  5. During the first five (5) seconds, display a title page on scene with mandatory background information.
  6. Start the raw video five (5) seconds into the film.
  7. Transition from the title scene to the video using the Cross Warp effect.
  8. Mute the audio on the raw video footage.
  9. Cut the following sections from the video in the same order listed here:
    1. 00:23.500 to 00:25.500
    2. 00:34.500 to 00:37.500
    3. 00:35.600 to 00:37.500
    4. 00:43.200 to 00:51.200
  10. Add a Zoom in transitional effect at 00:34.500.
  11. Annotate the film with grawlixe text (#$@&%*!) from 00:39.900 to 00:43.200.
  12. Show the outro scene with "The End!" text from 00:51.200 to 00:55.00.
  13. Transition to the outro with the Circle effect.
  14. Display the references page for the last five (5) seconds.
